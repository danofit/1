Type Three Design Work

Landscape with Type and Gradients


To achieve precise curves, I skillfully employed the anchor point tool, carefully shaping the intricate details found in the designs. Additionally, the pen tool proved indispensable in creating crisp, straight lines that enhanced the composition. The curves played a significant role in crafting a visually captivating sky and its reflection on the water, as well as artfully encircling the trees and mountains.


Initially presented in grayscale, the first picture provided a solid foundation for the subsequent step. Subsequently, by employing a color wheel in the second image, I expertly infused vibrant and harmonious hues into the scene, breathing life into the mountains and trees.


Type Pairing


The utilization of type in this context is a fascinating reflection of its purpose. The term "labels" was intentionally crafted to closely resemble the supplementary characters found in the Japanese language. Furthermore, the logo exudes a sense of sophistication and contemporariness, effectively embodying a serious or modern aesthetic.


Over the course of the past century, various fonts have been employed to transform the appearance of the logo, infusing it with a heightened level of seriousness or modernity. These specific fonts have long been associated with formal and legal applications, further accentuating the logo's professional and authoritative appeal.
